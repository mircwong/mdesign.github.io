An Introduction to Electric Transportation

In the past few years, commuters have transitioned over to electric transportation services. Ride-sharing giants, like Uber and Lyft, have answered this high demand by creating their own bike and scooter rental services. Not far behind, startups like Bird and Spin have also joined the game hoping to dominate the industry and are working to improve urban sustainability.

Through mobile app development, these companies are establishing themselves as primary electric transportation services. Without even lifting a muscle, commuters can easily open the company's app and rent a bike or scooter, get to where they need to be, tap a few buttons on their smartphones, and be on their way.

The advantages of using these new modes of transportation are that they are convenient, easily accessible, relatively quick, affordable, and reliable. In addition, integration of these transportation services into metropolitan and suburban areas limit urban congestion and emissions that contribute to smog and climate change. They also allow for alternative fuel sources to be fully and efficiently utilized.

While there are many benefits that come along with this mode of transportation, there are some problems that arise. It is a bit too early to predict whether or not this is the future of all transportation, but it is a revolutionary outbreak. Many people are pushing toward updating infrastructure to design for safer and more pleasant commutes by developing protected lanes, charging stations, and parking areas for electric transportation services.
